Omelette

Description

An omelette is a simple, folded egg dish cooked in a pan with butter or oil, often filled with ingredients like cheese, meats, or vegetables, and seasoned with salt and pepper, resulting in a quick, customizable meal that's either soft and custardy (French style) or more robust and fluffy (American style).

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Butter or extra-virgin olive oil
  • Desired fillings

Steps

  1. Crack your eggs into a small bowl.
  2. Whisk them vigorously until the whites and yolks fully combine — no streaks. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium-low heat. Add about 15g butter (or a swirl of olive oil) and let it melt without browning.
  4. Pour the eggs into the pan. Let them sit, untouched, for about 10 seconds to start setting.
  5. Then, with a silicone spatula, gently push the edges toward the center, tilting the pan so raw egg flows to the edges.
  6. Repeat this delicate push-and-pull for about a minute, until the omelet is mostly set but still slightly soft on top.
  7. Scatter your chosen filling over one half of the omelet.
  8. Use the spatula to gently fold the other half over the top — like closing a soft envelope
  9. Let it sit another 15–20 seconds to finish setting
  10. Slide it onto a plate, season lightly again if you want, and serve immediately.
